Creating a content workflow management system

Client: Simplus

Date: May 15, 2018

Skills: UX Design

Demo: Internal Use Only

Simplus manages the content marketing for a number of high-profile financial institutions. To manage the volume of content being created, it needed a workflow management system which enables it digitize their operational processes to efficiently coordinate with all their stake holders i.e. clients, sales team, writers, editors, and the leadership team.

The Problem

Co-ordinating each contracted piece of content, its feedback, revisions, formats, and delivery was creating endless trail mails. This process needed to be simplified to avoid miscommunication and minimise turnaround time.

Using a variety of platforms to co-ordinate the various activities, sharing files, feedback, etc was fragmenting the workflow.

With over a dozen clients pushing out hundreds of pieces of articles per week, it's an insane amount of emails we have to deal with in back-&-forth conversations - both internal and external.

Rajas Kelkar, CEO

The Constraints

The resulting solution was subject to the constraint that the learning curve should not be too steep. The WMS needed to hit the ground running, as existing projects would need to be onboarded.

As such, the design needed to be simple enough to require little to no training, but also allow enough flexibility to account for a wide variety of use cases, accommodating different deliverable types and client delivery and billing preferences.

Additionally, file handling for client delivery and approval needed to primarily be online, since majority of client devices prohibited downloading files.

Our Approach

First, we needed to understand the existing workflow/process Simplus followed and identify the exact pain points and areas for improvement.

Based on the action items identified, we then classified them into 2 categories – Must Have, and Nice To Have. This allowed us to prioritise which functionality we wanted to implement first.

The final solution needed to create a seamless workflow management system with a flexible content delivery system, sharing documents, information, or tasks between various stakeholders based on a defined set of rules to reduce costs, time taken, and maximise output.

My Process
